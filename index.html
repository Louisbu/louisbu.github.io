About Me

I am an M.Sc. Student in Mathematics under the supervision of Dr. Robert Milson, at Dalhousie University, Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada. I received my bachelors degree in mathematics with honours under Dr. Milson as well.

I am working with Canonical Green, a startup in Spain, Dr. David Goméz-Ullate (IE University), and my supervisor on developing algorithms that utilize wind, waves, currents, and other meteorological information to reduce fuel consumption and improve voyage safety for cargo ships.

I attended and presented at various conferences, such as the Science Atlantic Mathematics, Statistics, and Computer Science Conference and the Canadian Mathematics Society Winter Meeting 2023.

During my Mitacs Internship at Canonical Green in Madrid, Spain, I developed and improved the backend for computing and optimizing maritime transportation routes. I integrated optimization algorithms and improved the computation pipeline, presenting the results at the Science Atlantic MSCS 2023 conference. Additionally, I attended workshops and events related to environmental monitoring and collaborated with startup companies at the PIER Halifax.

I play some violin on my freetime, I also like Skiing, rollerblading, and playing video games.

Research Interests

I like reading about Differential Geometry and Manifold theory. I am also interested in Ordinary and Partial Differential Geometry, and their Asymptotic Solutions.

I have also looked into category theory, representation theory, and module theory from courses I have taken.
